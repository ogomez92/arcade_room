Tempest Tube
audio-first electric tube shooter

Introduction

Tempest Tube is an audio-first arcade shooter inspired by classic tube shooters. You ride the rim of a 16-lane tube, move left or right around the edge, and fire inward at enemies before they reach you.

The audio listener is camera-locked: front is always down the tube. Enemy lane audio rides your rim position: same-lane threats are centered, left/right tells you which way to move, and the fast radar ping confirms a clear shot. Tankers still need two hits.

System Requirements

This is best experienced with headphones and a gamepad.

Real-time synthesis is an extremely CPU-intensive process. It will attempt to run on any machine that meets its minimum requirements; however, you may experience stuttering or drop-outs when running below the recommended requirements.

Minimum System Requirements

CPU SSE2 capable (64-bit)
RAM 2 GB

Recommended System Requirements

CPU 3.5 GHz Quad-Core (64-bit)
RAM 4 GB

Browser Support

The HTML5 build is intended to be played in a recent version of a Chromium-based browser, such as Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, or Opera. Browser extensions that alter page behavior are not recommended. Other browsers must support modern JavaScript features and the current Web Audio API specification.

How to Play

Move around the rim and line up with a threat's lane. Fire inward to destroy it. Tankers take two hits and split into sparks in the two adjacent lanes; sparks are fast one-hit fragments. Spikers build lane spikes; clear spikes by shooting down that lane. Fuseballs jump lanes and become more common in later sectors.

Scoring rewards early shots: enemies destroyed deeper in the tube pay more. Destroy enemies within three seconds of each other to build combo multipliers from x2 up to x8. Tankers remember the farthest hit before they die, so an early long-range hit still helps the final score.

The run is an endless score chase. Your sector rises as your score increases, and higher sectors spawn faster, more varied threats. At game over, you can post your score to the world leaderboard; if the network is unavailable, local top-10 scores still work.
