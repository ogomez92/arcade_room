Mech Duel
Accessible audio combat

Introduction

Mech Duel is a 1v1 audio combat game. Two pilots fight inside a walled arena using distinct mechs with different movement and weapon profiles. Everything you need to play is conveyed through synthesized, spatialized sound and screen-reader announcements.

Use headphones for the best experience.

System Requirements

The HTML5 build runs in recent Chromium-based browsers (Chrome, Edge, Opera). The Electron build ships with the same engine.

How to play

  1. From the main menu, pick Play vs computer, Host an online duel, or Join an online duel.
  2. Choose a mech. Use Left / Right to cycle and P to preview the engine sound.
  3. Destroy the opposing mech before yours is destroyed. Match ends the moment someone reaches zero health.

Reading the arena by ear

  • Engine hum: you always hear your own engine (pitched by throttle) and the opponent's (spatialized — it gets louder and pans when they are close).
  • Footsteps: legged mechs thud while walking; jetpack mechs do not.
  • Wall radar: a parking-aid beep. Front walls beep high, side walls mid, rear walls low. The closer you get, the faster the beeps.
  • Aim sonar: continuous beeps that get louder, higher, and faster as your facing lines up with the opponent and they are within your weapon's range. Switch range reference with Shift+F (primary) or Shift+R (secondary).
  • Voice announcements: damage events, collisions, and status are read aloud via the screen-reader live region.
  • Learn game sounds from the main menu plays every distinct cue so you can practice recognizing them before you fight.

Tips

  • Cardinal snap is the fastest way to line up a shot with sonar guidance — press Shift + ←/→ and listen for the cardinal name.
  • Jetpack mechs can land on an opponent for a massive fall-attack bonus.
  • Against the Juggernaut, keep moving — its rail cannon hurts but reloads slowly.
  • Listen for the engine pitch: a stopped mech is silent from the drive train, but footsteps can still give it away.
  • If a wall radar beep suddenly turns high-pitch, you're driving nose-first into the wall — slow down.
